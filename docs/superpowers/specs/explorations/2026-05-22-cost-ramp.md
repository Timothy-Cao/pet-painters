# Exploration: Per-Pet Cost Ramp

**Date:** 2026-05-22
**Status:** Deferred — revisit after first real-player playtests

---

## The Idea

Each copy of pet X that a player deploys costs **+10% more** than the previous copy deployed that round.

Concretely: the first Cat costs its base energy cost (e.g. 12). The second Cat costs 12 × 1.1 = 13. The third costs 12 × 1.21 = 14 (rounded). And so on.

The multiplier resets to 1.0 at the start of each round.

Variant: use a flat +X additive cost instead of percentage, e.g. first Cat = 12, second = 18, third = 24. Simpler to explain.

---

## Motivation

During balance testing the sim routinely finds "just spam three Spiders" comps outperforming diverse comps. The cost ramp would nudge players toward variety without banning multi-copies outright. It also adds a **poker-like signal**: if an opponent sees you queued two Rabbits, they know you paid extra, and can deduce your resource situation.

---

## Pros

- **Encourages variety.** Two Mice + one Lion is cheaper than three Mice.
- **Soft swarm nerf.** Spam comps still work but at a cost, making them a deliberate choice rather than the dominant default.
- **Legible.** Players quickly build intuition: "stacking same pets = expensive."
- **Low friction to prototype.** Implementation is ~30 min (see below).

---

## Cons

- **Breaks swarm identities.** The Rhino's narrative is "one big tank" and costs little to triple up. The Spider's narrative is "web-field controller" that benefits from three copies (triangulated webs). A blanket cost ramp undercuts those identities.
- **Punishes natural counter-picks.** If the counter to Dragon is two Mouses, the ramp penalizes the correct answer.
- **Confusion.** Showing a player that "Mouse costs 8, or 9, or 10 depending on how many you've deployed" requires extra UI. Without clear feedback the ramp is invisible until it stings.
- **Sim invalidation.** All existing balance numbers were tuned without a cost ramp. Enabling it would require re-running the balance sweep.

---

## Implementation Cost (~30 minutes)

1. Add `deployedThisRound: Record<string, number>` to `MatchState` (per-player).
2. In `tryDeploy` (`src/sim/deploy.ts`): before spending energy, compute
   `effectiveCost = Math.ceil(baseCost * (1.1 ** deployedThisRound[player][defId] ?? 0))`.
3. Increment `deployedThisRound[player][defId]` on successful deploy.
4. Reset counters in `submitReady` (or `endExecution`) when planning starts.
5. Show effective cost in the pet roster card (small orange badge when cost has ramped).

The sim engine change is contained to `src/sim/deploy.ts` and `src/types/game.ts` — about 15 lines total.

---

## Decision: Defer to Post-Playtest

**Why defer:** Real players may pick variety naturally (because diverse comps are more interesting to play) without needing a financial nudge. If they do spam, the existing energy budget already limits burst; and the balance team's per-round counters handle specific spam threats.

The risk of removing swarm identity before seeing real play is higher than the risk of leaving it in.

**Trigger to revisit:** If two or more consecutive alpha sessions end with one player fielding 3+ of the same pet and winning convincingly, ship the cost ramp. Otherwise keep energy budget as the sole cost lever.
